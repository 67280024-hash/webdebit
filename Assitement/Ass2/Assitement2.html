Pad Ka Prao

Ingredients

  • bulb Garlic
  • cup Holy Basil leaves
  • Green Cayenne Peppers (optional)
  • Red Chili Peppers
  • Fish Sauce
  • Mushroom Soy Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Oil
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Water or Unsalted broth

How to Make Pad Ka Prao : Step by Step

  • 1.Slice green cayenne peppers. Peel the garlic. Pound chilies and garlic** in a mortar and pestle (if you don’t have a mortar and pestle, just put them in a zip-lock bag and pound them with a heavy stuff or knife). They should be fine enough to give you the oils and flavors.
  • 2.Heat a pan on medium high heat. Add oil to the pan. When the oil is hot enough, add the chilies and garlic. Stir-fry until they get fragrant. Add the green cayenne peppers.
  • 3.Turn the heat up higher. Add the meat to the pan. Stir-fry the meat for 5-10 minutes (depending on the amount of meat) or until it’s about cooked. If it starts getting dry, add small amount of water(a tiny bit).
  • 4.Add fish sauce, (mushroom) soy sauce, sugar, oyster sauce and chicken bouillon(optional). Keep stirring until it’s mixed well. Taste and seasoning as necessary.
  • 5.Toss holy basil into the pan and fold it into the meat. Don’t cook it for too long. Now it’s ready to be served!

Pad Krapow option

Pad Krapow doesn’t need meat at all the dish is incredibly flexible, and the choice of protein can completely change the flavor, aroma, and texture. If you prefer a traditional style that highlights the fragrance of garlic, chili, and holy basil, ground pork or ground chicken are the classic choices. They absorb sauce well and create a balanced, savory base. Ground beef gives a deeper, richer flavor for those who enjoy stronger tastes. Crispy pork belly has become very popular because it adds a crunchy texture and a naturally rich aroma that elevates the whole dish. Seafood options like shrimp or squid bring a natural sweetness and a lighter, fresher profile while still pairing beautifully with basil and chili..
